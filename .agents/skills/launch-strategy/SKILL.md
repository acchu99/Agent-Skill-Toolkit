# Launch Strategy

> A launch is a moment, not a milestone. The product should already work; the launch concentrates attention on it.

## When to Apply

New product, major feature, public beta → GA, rebrand, or a marketing moment you want to manufacture (report, tool, integration). Scale the plan to the stakes — a feature ship needs a changelog + email; a company launch needs the full playbook.

## Positioning First

Before any channel or asset, lock the message:

| Element | One sentence |
|---------|--------------|
| **For whom** | The specific audience who feels the problem most. |
| **The problem** | What's broken today, in their words. |
| **The category** | What kind of thing this is (sets expectations). |
| **The differentiator** | Why this, not the obvious alternative. |
| **The proof** | The single most credible piece of evidence. |

If you can't fill this table, you're not ready to launch — you're ready to iterate.

## Three Phases

### Pre-launch (weeks before)
- Build an **audience to launch to**: waitlist, email list, engaged community, design partners.
- Line up **proof**: testimonials, case study, metrics, a demo that works flawlessly.
- Prepare **assets** (below) and get **early access** into a few hands for quotes.
- Warm up channels: tease, build-in-public posts, DM key people for launch-day support.

### Launch day
- Publish in a **deliberate sequence**, not all at once: owned channels first (email, blog), then the platform you're betting on, then amplification.
- **Be present**: respond to every comment/question fast — engagement drives ranking on PH/HN/Reddit.
- Don't ask for upvotes on platforms that ban it (HN, PH); ask people to "check it out".

### Post-launch (days–weeks after)
- Follow up with people who engaged; convert attention into activation.
- Publish a **recap** with numbers (build-in-public credibility compounds).
- Feed learnings back into the product and the next launch.
- Hand off to operations — see `operator-launch-handoff`.

## Channel Selection

Pick channels by **where your audience already is**, not by reach.

| Channel | Best for | Watch out |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| **Product Hunt** | Tools, dev/prosumer products | One shot; needs day-of hustle; timezone matters |
| **Hacker News** | Dev tools, technical depth | No self-promo tone; "Show HN"; substance only |
| **Reddit** | Niche communities | Must be a genuine member; read subreddit rules |
| **X / LinkedIn** | Build-in-public, B2B | Needs an existing following or paid amplification |
| **Email list** | Highest intent, you own it | Only as good as the list you built pre-launch |
| **Communities/Slack/Discord** | Targeted, high-trust | No spamming; contribute first |
| **Press/newsletters** | Reach + credibility | Long lead time; needs a real hook |

> Owned channels (email, existing users) convert best. Rented channels (PH, social) drive reach. Do both, owned first.

## Launch-Asset Checklist

- [ ] One-line pitch (the positioning differentiator)
- [ ] Landing page with a single primary CTA (see `conversion-rate-optimization`)
- [ ] Demo (video and/or live) that works without narration
- [ ] Screenshots / GIFs / hero visual
- [ ] Launch post per channel (tailored, not copy-pasted)
- [ ] Email to list (see `copywriting`)
- [ ] Social thread / build-in-public post
- [ ] FAQ + objection answers ready for comments
- [ ] Pricing page finalized (see `pricing-strategy`)
- [ ] Analytics/tracking in place before traffic arrives

## Success Metrics

Define what "good" means before launch day so you're not rationalizing after:

| Metric | Signal |
|--------|--------|
| Traffic / signups | Reach and top-of-funnel interest |
| Activation rate | Did new users reach first value? (the real one) |
| Ranking/placement | PH position, HN front page, engagement rate |
| Qualitative | Comments, DMs, feature requests — the "why" |
| Retention (week 1) | Whether the launch attracted the right users |

Vanity (upvotes, impressions) is a leading indicator; activation and retention are the ones that matter.

## Anti-Patterns

❌ **Launching a broken product** — attention on a bad first impression is negative.
❌ **No audience to launch to** — building the list on launch day is too late.
❌ **Spray-and-pray** — every channel, same copy, low effort each.
❌ **Ghosting the comments** — engagement is the launch; showing up is the work.
❌ **No metrics defined** — you can't tell if it worked.
